# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary reader is a technical peer: fellow developers and builders in the homelab, AI-agents, and self-hosting world who will read the technical writing and recognise the craft behind the site itself. A serious visitor evaluating Richard's work (a prospective client or employer) is the confident secondary audience, served by the portfolio without the site ever tilting into a sales pitch. Both arrive curious rather than task-driven; nobody *needs* to be here, so the site has to earn the read.

## Product Purpose

richardkern.nz is Richard's personal site: a blog spanning technical material (homelab, AI agents, web development) and personal reflection (running, aviation, building things for their own sake), alongside a portfolio of what he has built. The two halves are deliberate, not scope drift. Success is not a metric. The site succeeds by existing and being genuinely well made: a place that is simply good, a credible home for the writing and a record worth keeping. If it draws opportunities, that is a welcome side effect, not the goal it is optimised for.

## Positioning

A curious, careful mind at work. The throughline is not the subject matter but the attention brought to it: the same rigour and care runs through a post on AI agents, a note on a run, and the design of the page it sits on. The value on offer is the thinking, not the topic.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: get in touch. Offered quietly through the social links in the spine and footer, never pushed with popups or interstitials.
- Secondary path: the RSS feed and social links, for a visitor who wants to follow rather than reach out yet.
- The line a visitor should remember after ten seconds: this person makes careful, considered things, and this site is one of them.
- Proof on hand: none formal, by design. The writing and the portfolio are the proof; the craft of the site is corroborating evidence. No testimonials or logos.

## Brand Personality

Understated, precise, warm. Quiet confidence rather than announcement: nothing shouts, and the care shows in the details. The voice is a real person thinking in plain prose, direct and unhurried, human rather than clinical or performative.

## Anti-references

The site must not read as any of these:

- The generic dev-portfolio template: dark-mode neon-gradient hero, skill bars, "Hi, I'm X, Full-Stack Developer."
- The SaaS or startup marketing site: cream backgrounds, tracked-uppercase eyebrows, hero-metric grids, CTA-stuffed landing pages.
- The loud personal-brand influencer: big face, hustle energy, newsletter-popup-first, monetised-everything.
- The sterile corporate blog: faceless, over-templated, no point of view.

## Design Principles

- Craft is the argument. The site earns trust by being well made, not by claiming to be. How it is built is itself part of what it says.
- One voice across every subject. Technical posts and personal reflection get the same careful attention; the coherence is the mind at work, not a stated theme, so nothing should feel like a different site when the topic changes.
- Understatement over announcement. Confidence is shown, not declared. When a choice could be loud or quiet, choose quiet.
- Design is the product. This is a brand surface: the reading experience itself is the value, so legibility, rhythm, and typographic care are not decoration, they are the substance.
- Offer, don't sell. Getting in touch is made easy and left available; the work and the writing do the persuading, never a hard prompt.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target is committed. In practice the built site already clears a responsible bar: theme-aware tokens sized for contrast, `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured (the one home-reveal moment falls back to nothing), a real light/dark theme following the OS with a persisted override, and `color-scheme` declared so mobile browsers stop force-darkening the page. Keep new work at that standard; treat AA contrast and keyboard operability as the default even without a stated commitment.
